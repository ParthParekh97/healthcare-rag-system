Title: Chronic Kidney Disease: Staging, Management, and Referral Guidelines
Source: Kidney Disease Improving Global Outcomes (KDIGO) 2024 Clinical Practice Guidelines
Document ID: HC-004

1. Definition and Staging

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is defined as abnormalities of kidney structure or function present for more than 3 months with implications for health. CKD is staged based on estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR) and albuminuria:

eGFR-Based Staging:
- Stage G1: eGFR >= 90 mL/min/1.73m2 (Normal or high) with evidence of kidney damage
- Stage G2: eGFR 60-89 mL/min/1.73m2 (Mildly decreased) with evidence of kidney damage
- Stage G3a: eGFR 45-59 mL/min/1.73m2 (Mildly to moderately decreased)
- Stage G3b: eGFR 30-44 mL/min/1.73m2 (Moderately to severely decreased)
- Stage G4: eGFR 15-29 mL/min/1.73m2 (Severely decreased)
- Stage G5: eGFR < 15 mL/min/1.73m2 (Kidney failure)

Albuminuria Categories:
- A1: UACR < 30 mg/g (Normal to mildly increased)
- A2: UACR 30-300 mg/g (Moderately increased, formerly "microalbuminuria")
- A3: UACR > 300 mg/g (Severely increased, formerly "macroalbuminuria")

2. Epidemiology

CKD affects approximately 15% of the US adult population (37 million people). Leading causes are diabetes (44%) and hypertension (29%). CKD is associated with a 2-3 fold increased risk of cardiovascular mortality. Many patients with early CKD are undiagnosed, making screening in high-risk populations essential.

3. Screening Recommendations

Annual screening is recommended for high-risk populations:
- Patients with diabetes mellitus
- Patients with hypertension
- Family history of kidney disease
- Age > 60 years
- History of acute kidney injury
- Cardiovascular disease
- Obesity
- Certain ethnic groups (African American, Hispanic, Native American)

Screening tests: serum creatinine with eGFR calculation (using CKD-EPI 2021 equation without race coefficient), urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR).

4. Management by Stage

Stage G1-G2 (eGFR >= 60 with kidney damage):
- Identify and treat underlying cause
- Blood pressure control (target < 130/80 mmHg)
- ACE inhibitor or ARB for patients with albuminuria (UACR >= 30 mg/g)
- SGLT2 inhibitor (dapagliflozin or empagliflozin) if UACR >= 200 mg/g or eGFR 20-45, regardless of diabetes status
- Statin therapy for cardiovascular risk reduction
- Annual monitoring of eGFR and UACR

Stage G3a-G3b (eGFR 30-59):
- All above measures plus:
- Monitor for CKD complications: anemia, metabolite bone disorder, acidosis, hyperkalemia
- Adjust medication dosing for kidney function
- Avoid nephrotoxic agents (NSAIDs, iodinated contrast without precautions, aminoglycosides)
- Phosphate management if serum phosphate elevated
- Bicarbonate supplementation if serum bicarbonate < 22 mEq/L
- Monitor parathyroid hormone (PTH) levels
- Monitoring every 3-6 months

Stage G4 (eGFR 15-29):
- All above measures plus:
- Nephrology referral required
- Renal replacement therapy education (hemodialysis, peritoneal dialysis, kidney transplantation, conservative management)
- Vascular access planning for hemodialysis (ideally AV fistula creation when eGFR approaches 15-20)
- Transplant evaluation if candidate
- Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents (ESAs) for anemia (target hemoglobin 10-11.5 g/dL)
- Dietary protein restriction (0.6-0.8 g/kg/day)
- Monitoring every 1-3 months

Stage G5 (eGFR < 15):
- Initiate renal replacement therapy when symptomatic or eGFR < 5-7 mL/min/1.73m2
- Indications for urgent dialysis: refractory hyperkalemia, severe metabolic acidosis, volume overload unresponsive to diuretics, uremic pericarditis, uremic encephalopathy

5. SGLT2 Inhibitors in CKD

Landmark trials have established SGLT2 inhibitors as foundational CKD therapy:
- DAPA-CKD trial: Dapagliflozin reduced composite kidney outcome by 39% vs placebo
- EMPA-KIDNEY trial: Empagliflozin reduced kidney progression by 28%
- Benefits independent of diabetes status
- Recommended for eGFR >= 20 mL/min/1.73m2 with UACR >= 200 mg/g
- Can be continued once initiated even if eGFR falls below 20
- Expected initial eGFR dip of 3-5 mL/min is hemodynamic and reversible

6. Cardiovascular Risk Management

CKD patients are at markedly elevated cardiovascular risk. Management includes:
- Statin or statin/ezetimibe combination for all CKD patients aged >= 50
- Aspirin for secondary prevention of atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease
- Blood pressure control with RAAS inhibitors as first-line
- GLP-1 receptor agonists for CKD patients with type 2 diabetes and high cardiovascular risk (based on FLOW trial data)
- Finerenone (nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist) for diabetic kidney disease with persistent albuminuria despite RAAS inhibitor therapy

7. Referral to Nephrology

Prompt nephrology referral is indicated for:
- eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m2 (Stage G4-G5)
- Persistent UACR > 300 mg/g
- Rapid eGFR decline (> 5 mL/min/1.73m2 per year)
- CKD with refractory hypertension (>= 4 agents)
- Persistent hyperkalemia (K > 5.5 mEq/L)
- Recurrent nephrolithiasis
- Suspected glomerulonephritis or hereditary kidney disease
